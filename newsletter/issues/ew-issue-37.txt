** What&#x27;s Coming In Ember In 2014
------------------------------------------------------------

It looks like 2014 is going to be an exciting year for Ember. The blog post covers the discussions and results of the core team&#x27;s face-to-face meeting last week. Highlights include tooling (build tools, modules, inspector, etc), HTMLBars, distributing components, emberdart, and innovative ways to open a beer. (http://emberjs.com/blog/2013/12/17/whats-coming-in-ember-in-2014.html)

** ES6 modules and EmberJS: a taste of the future (part 2)
------------------------------------------------------------

A follow up to [part 1](http://thau.me/2013/12/es6-modules-and-emberjs-a-taste-of-the-future-part-1/) which looked at ES6 modules, this post looks closer at how ES6 modules are used in Ember App Kit (EAK) as well as how EAK can simplify building Ember applications. (http://thau.me/2013/12/es6-modules-and-emberjs-a-taste-of-the-future-part-2/)


** Ember Inspector on a Firefox near you
------------------------------------------------------------

(...or Cross-Browser Add-ons for Fun or Profit). An interesting article where the Ember inspector takes centre stage in a detailed account of porting the Chrome version to Firefox. Learn not only how to write cross-browser extensions but how the Ember Inspector is built. (https://hacks.mozilla.org/2013/12/ember-inspector-on-a-firefox-near-you-2/)

(https://www.firebase.com/blog/2013-12-16-emberfire-guest-blog.html)

** Introducing Fireplace
------------------------------------------------------------

[Fireplace](https://github.com/rlivsey/fireplace) is a Firebase library for Ember that is more akin to [Ember Model](https://github.com/ebryn/ember-model) or Ember Data than the official [EmberFire](https://github.com/firebase/emberFire). Also see the video from the December Ember London meetup above. (http://livsey.org/blog/2013/12/13/introducing-fireplace/)
